Daily care

Food

What
Homemade meals. Half a container or block per meal.
How
Microwave 30 seconds. Longer if still frozen. Test the temp before giving it to him. Add a rice block every few days if you'd like.
Where
Freezer and fridge.
When
Twice a day. Morning around 6–8a, evening around 5p.
Treats
In the jar on the counter. No people food. No chicken treats.
Water
Fresh tap, top up daily, full refresh every 2–3 days.

Walks

How many
At least three. First thing in the morning, before dinner, before bed.
Length
Depends on temperature. Never longer than 45 minutes or you'll be carrying him home.
Routes
Arizona to 3rd Street, or Ocean depending on weather and time. Then up to Santa Monica or Broadway and back. 15–30 minutes is plenty.
Gear
Leash and harness on the door handle in the entry. Poop bags out front. Pepper spray and key in the drawer under the Buddha.
Heat rule
Frenchies overheat fast.

Under 65°F: longer walks are fine.

70°F and up: keep walks under 30 minutes.

Over 75°F: just out front is fine. Stick to shade. Watch the pavement, it burns paws.

If he pants and stops, listen. He's telling you he's done.

Louis · Do & Don't

Do

  • Lift him with a scoop. Always support his back end and legs.
  • Take him to see his friend Kellie at Sweat Yoga downstairs. He'll lead you.
  • Take him to the roof. He loves it up there.
  • Take him on errands: Vons, Target, Couplet, Chipotle, Monica. He's a scene guy.
  • Carry the pepper spray. Frenchie thefts are a thing. Be aware when strangers want to pet him.

Don't

  • Lift him by just the front end. It strains his back and can damage his spine.
  • Take him to the pier or the beach.
  • Take him for car rides.
  • Play too rough. He gets overstimulated and goes "crazy eyes." When that happens, walk away. He'll reset.
iv.

Watch for

Frenchies are brachycephalic, which means flat-faced and breathing-vulnerable. Most of the time he's fine. These are the things that warrant a call.

Overheating

Heavy panting that doesn't slow. Gums turning blue or bright red. Lethargy. Collapse. Cool him down immediately and call the vet.

Breathing distress

Different from his normal snorting. If he's struggling for air, especially after exertion or in heat, treat as emergency.

Eye issues

Cloudiness, excessive tearing, squinting, redness. Frenchies have prominent eyes and they're vulnerable.

Skin folds

Redness, smell, or wetness in his face folds.

GI

Vomiting more than once. Diarrhea more than a day. Refusing food.

When in doubt

Call or text Nik. There's no such thing as too soon.
